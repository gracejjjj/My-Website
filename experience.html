DATA ARCHITECTURE & DEVELOPMENT INTERN

Draper

Summer 2024

This past summer, I was a full time intern on the Data Architecture and Development team at Draper Labs in Cambridge, MA. The Data Arch & Dev team is responsible for designing, building and maintaining Draper's proprietary databases. I was working primarily on software development in Kotlin, a relatively new Java derivative. I contributed to a project to refactor the existing data dictionary for the largest of the proprietary databases, containing over 30,000 parameters. I learned a ton about the software development process - version control, testing code robustly, code pipelines, CI/CD, building and using utilities and libraries. I worked on twelve different merge requests that were all successfully merged and will be deployed in the next software release. My biggest takeaway from the internship is how important organization, communication and foresight is in the software development process - a software artifact must be well defined and well understood by everyone involved in the development process. I saw several examples of shortcuts and quick fixes in the past leading to technical debt that my team now has to handle.

MATH RESEARCH INTERN

New York City Math Alliance

Summer 2024

For Summer 2023, I participated in a Math Research Program with the New York City Math Alliance. I worked with a group of two other students to study various open questions about the commuting graphs of various matrix groups and rings. The research culminated in a paper proving a theorem we created about the diameter of commuting graphs over the group of matrices over Z mod n. The program was an opportunity to experience completely self-directed work with no concrete solution or desired outcome, and was a great opportunity to study the material covered in many of my college courses on a much deeper, theoretical level. Most of our work dealt with Abstract and Linear Algebra, and also involved programming in C++ and Python in order to model the commuting graphs that we were studying. It was very cool to be able to use my computer science background to automate these visuals in my math research! See the projects tab for a few examples of these diagrams and a high level description of our theorem.

MACHINE LEARNING STUDY ABROAD PROGRAM

DIS Abroad

January 2024 - May 2024

This past Spring, I had the opportunity to spend a semester abroad in Stockholm, Sweden and was accepted to a program with a focus in Machine Learning. Over the course of the program, we learned about the mathematical theory behind common machine learning models, as well as how to build them using Python libraries and various datasets. As a math and computer science double major, I found this material super interesting as it lies at the direct intersection of my two major interests. The program had weekly field studies where we visited companies and research institutions who were using machine learning to create cutting edge technologies in various industries including medicine, sports performance, law and others. Our professor was earning her PhD in Machine Learning and AI Ethics, so we spent a portion of the semester studying the ethics of various AI tools and how to combat biases that are often embedded in these powerful models. The semester culminated in a final project that included building predictive models using a dataset of our choice and evaluating their performance and ethical implications. It was an incredible experience and definitely left me wanting to learn more about building these powerful models and implementing them in ethical and responsible ways.

MODERN ALGEBRA TEACHING ASSISTANT

Hamilton College

December 2022 - present

For the past two years, I have served as the teaching assistant for the Modern Algbebra Mathematics course. Modern Algebra is an advanced level proofwriting class and is a requirement for all math majors. This job requires that I hold weekly TA hours during which I am available to students to answer questions on homeworks or exam prep. It also involves grading and leaving feedback on biweekly computational homework assignments. This position has been more valuable than I knew when I first accepted the job. After having some industry experience in technical roles, I have learned that being able to convey complex technical ideas to a nontechnical peer is a hugely valuable skill. Whether it be communicating an idea to a manager, marketing team or customer, being able to comprehensibly describe complex ideas in simple terms is essential to success in a technical role. In this role, I had to learn how to assess and cater to each student's needs and current understanding, and convey concepts in a way that would be most beneficial to them specifically.
